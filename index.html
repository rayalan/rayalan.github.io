Welcome to Binary Adages¶

Ah, computers. They made the world go around, and yet they are so literal. We spend years learning how to tell them what to do. And then they fail with spectacular blue screens and useless messages like “I’m sorry Dave, I can’t do that.” or “Null pointer error at address 0x0088001B.”

Here, I write about a variety of the tips, tricks, and idiosyncrasies I encounter as a developer. Why do I write? Primarily because:

  1. To contribute
In the course of developing, I’ve benefited immensely from other developers who have posted their arcane knowledge. And from authors like Joel Spolsky whose articles have shaped my view of programming. Hopefully other developers while find portions of my writings similarly useful.
  1. For personal documentation
There’s a deep irksome quality to solving a hard problem or bug and then but then losing the key insights that led to that solution. If it was worth solving once, it should stay solved forever. Or until someone pays me several million dollars to bury the solution.
  1. To learn reStructuredText and Sphinx
One of my goals is to get better at documenting code, as part of that, I’m learning Sphinx and reStructuredText. Since I learn better through usage and encountering real problems, this blog should give some good learning opportunities.
